# Trader Behavior Analysis based on Market Sentiment

## 1. Introduction

This project analyzes the relationship between trader performance and market sentiment (Fear vs Greed). The objective is to identify patterns that can help in making better trading decisions.

## 2. Dataset Description

Two datasets were used:

* Historical trader data containing trade details such as profit/loss, leverage, and execution time.
* Bitcoin Fear & Greed Index dataset indicating market sentiment.

## 3. Methodology

* Cleaned and standardized both datasets.
* Converted timestamps into a common date format.
* Merged datasets based on date.
* Analyzed trader performance across different sentiments.

## 4. Key Insights

* Traders tend to perform better during **Greed** market conditions.
* Losses are more frequent during **Fear** due to panic-driven decisions.
* High leverage increases risk significantly during Fear periods.
* Buy trades generally perform better in Greed markets.

## 5. Conclusion

Market sentiment plays a crucial role in trading performance. Traders who adapt their strategies based on sentiment can improve profitability and reduce risks.
